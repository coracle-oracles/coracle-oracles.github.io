Survival Guide

Radical Inclusion

Anyone may be a part of Burning Man. We welcome and respect the stranger. No prerequisites exist for participation in our community.

About the Venue

This year Coracle will be held at Shakori Hills in Pittsboro, NC. Shakori Hills Community Arts Center is located at 1439 Henderson Tanyard Road in Chatham County, North Carolina in the fire district of Silk Hope–14 miles northeast of Siler City and 18 miles west of Chapel Hill. Shakori Hills is a beautiful farm of rolling hills and meadows in the very heart of North Carolina.

Gate Hours

  • Early Entry for Theme Camps and Art Installations begins at 12pm on Friday, Nov. 1st
  • Early Gate closes (no more entries) for early entry at 8pm on Friday, Nov. 1st
  • Coracle Entry gates open at 10am on Saturday, Nov. 2nd
  • Coracle Gate closes (no more entries) at 6pm Saturday, Nov. 2nd
  • Coracle ends at 5pm Sunday, Nov. 3rd
  • Coracle build team ends at 8pm on Sunday, Nov. 3rd

Early Entry

You must be cleared with the Coracle production team before planning to arrive on Friday, Nov. 1st. The early gate opens at 12 noon and closes at 8pm on Friday, Nov. 1st.

Coracle Entry

Bring a valid government-issued photo ID and your ticket, either printed or a screenshot in case your phone does not have service. Check in at the gate. If at all possible, pre-print and sign your waiver and have it ready at the gate. (You will be required to sign a release waiver.)

General Survival

You must bring enough food, water, shelter, prescriptions, etc. to survive in the environment while attending Coracle. Bring a fork, cup, spoon, and knife.

At your Own Risk

Coracle is an “At Your Own Risk” event. You will be required to sign a release waiver before entering Coracle.

Wrist Bands

This is a private event on private property. If you have lost your wristband, you should go to the Front Gate immediately to get a new one. You will need your ID to verify your original ticket purchase. Anyone seen without a wristband will be escorted to the gate. If you do not comply with wristband protocol you are trespassing on private property, and we will involve law enforcement.

Medical Emergencies

There are no medical services onsite at Coracle. 9-1-1 must be called in the case of a medical emergency. Cell phone service is spotty to non-existent at the venue. If your cell phone does not have service, please find one of the Coracle organizers for access to a landline.

Buddy System

Find a buddy and do not wander off by yourself. Shakori Hills is a very large property and we are an intimate group. There are no fences inside. Make sure that you are staying in visible areas and have a buddy. Make sure your children are in your sight at all times.

Children

All children under the age of 18 attending Coracle must have a ticket and be accompanied by an adult. You will be asked to fill out an additional child registration form at the gate upon arrival.

Lost Child

Guardians, please keep your children within sight at all times. In the case of a lost child, go to the stage onsite so an amplified announcement can be made. Find someone with a radio to report the lost child so we can spread the message to others onsite.

Nudity

This is a clothing optional event in designated areas. It is your personal responsibility to remove yourself, or any minors accompanying you, from the area of any behavior you find offensive. Or, you may leave the event. Obscenity is prohibited by North Carolina state law and is not allowed at any time.

Physical and Emotional Self-Care

Make sure you are adequately fed, rested and hydrated. Make sure your clothes are adequate for the weather (check the weather before the event). Make sure you are wearing appropriate shoes for the environment. Remove yourself from a situation if you are feeling emotionally triggered. If you are experiencing a mental or emotional emergency ask for help.

Temperature

The average temperature the weekend of Coracle (Nov. 2-3rd, 2019) ranges from 63° during the day to 37° at night. The temperature at night has gotten as low as 28 degrees in past years. Bring very warm bedding and coats. Hand warmers and foot warmers are recommended.

Rain

It often rains at Coracle. Be prepared with rain gear including rain boots and jackets.

Flora and Fauna

Snakes, rodents, spiders, bees, ticks, poison ivy/oak/sumac are present on the site of Shakori Hills. Please prepare with tick repellent, tecnu and any other protection you require. Do a full body tick check before going to bed.

Fire

There are designated fire areas onsite. If you would like to bring a fire barrel or similar for your theme camp, it needs to be off the ground and ashes should be carried out or put into the designated fire pit. Fires ARE NOT permitted in individual campsites due to tree cover, please enjoy fires in designated areas.

Fire Dancing

Fire performance is at your own risk. You must carry your own liability insurance. Fire performers must be clear of vegetation and other flammables, and need to have a bucket of water / damp towel handy AND a safety person watching out for you.

Effigy Burn

You will be instructed to stay back when the effigy begins to burn on Saturday night. Follow all instructions from the perimeter team for your own safety. Please respect the community by keeping you and your family safe around the effigy fire.

Flame Effects

Your flame effects must be inspected by the Fire Marshall on Friday, Nov. 1st in order to operate them at Coracle. You must have a fire extinguisher. Operating flame effects is at your own risk. Those bringing flame effects take full responsibility for their safe operation, and prevention of damage to venue property. Most importantly, you take on the responsibility of operating them in a way that is free from unnecessary danger to Coracle participants. For participants enjoying flame effects, please respect the community by keeping you and your family safe around flame effects.

Generators

If you or your theme camp use a generator you must have a fire extinguisher onsite.

Sound / Noise

Our noise permit allows up to 80 decibels of sound from 7am - 11pm on Friday, Nov. 1st, Saturday, Nov. 2nd and Sunday, Nov. 3rd (until 9pm). After 11pm on Friday and Saturday night it is up to the discretion of the neighbors to deem our noise too loud, but we are never to exceed 80 decibels of sound at any time. Please use best judgement after 11pm and turn down your voice and any amplified sound to a respectful level.

Camping

There is a camping area onsite. There are also spaces for RV’s. There are no RV hookups available onsite. You will need to purchase a Car Pass for each vehicle and an RV pass for your RV or trailer. Fires aren’t permitted in individual campsites due to tree cover, please enjoy fires in designated areas.

Bathrooms

There are bathrooms with running water, and Portos onsite for your convenience. Only toilet paper down the toilet. Please bring a bucket/camping potty/pee jug if you or your family are not comfortable taking a walk to the bathrooms located onsite.

Drinking Water & Showers

Drinking water stations provide cold well water. Please bring a refillable container. There are warm showers and running water bathrooms onsite. Bring a towel if you plan to use the showers.

Food

You should plan to bring whatever delicious delights you would like to enjoy at Coracle. You can bring grills and camping stoves. There is no refrigeration available but a simple cooler should do given the Fall chill. All food waste needs to be carried out in compost or trash. Leftover cooler ice should be scattered (not piled up) or taken home. You must bring your own plates, silverware, cups, etc. with you, as these will not be provided. The onsite kitchen is NOT available for your individual use.

Volunteer Feast

On Saturday night, we feast! This is a Coracle tradition and open to all event volunteers (that's you!). We gather Saturday night to honor our community effort and each other through a shared meal. No contribution is necessary. Dinner is served around 8pm. Bring your own plate, bowl, utensils, and beverages.

Alcohol/Theme Camp Bars

Want to make a round of drinks for other participants? Hooray! HOWEVER you are responsible for ensuring only those 21+ are served by checking their photo i.d. There is absolutely no serving of alcohol to persons under 21 years of age at Coracle. If such activities occur you will be asked to leave immediately.

Leaving No Trace (LNT)

Pack it in, pack it out. There is no communal trash or recycling. Please bring bags for both trash and recycling. This is a “Leave No Trace” (LNT) event and all trash must be removed prior to departure. Shakori Hills encourages composting, a composting bucket will be provided by our LNT team - no oils, dairy, or meat. If your camp is serving food or drinks to other participants then any plates, utensils, etc. that you provide must be compostable or reusable, per the request of Shakori Hills. A sharps container (for disposal of sharp or hazardous waste) will also be provided. You must clean your theme camp, art piece and campsite before departing Coracle.

Illegal Drugs

Consumption, possession, or distribution of illegal substances constitutes a criminal act and the violation is subject to immediate ejection from Coracle, in addition to criminal penalties.

Decommodification

Vending and other commercial activity is strictly prohibited at Coracle and is grounds for immediate ejection.

Firearms, Ammunition, Projectile Weapons, Fireworks, Explosives

You are strictly prohibited from bringing firearms, ammunition, projectile weapons, fireworks or explosives of any type to Coracle, and you may not have any such prohibited items anywhere on the premises including in your vehicle.

Consent

Please be absolutely sure that you have a resounding YES from any other participants before doing anything that involves them or you will be asked to leave Coracle immediately. Our community is based on respect for one another.

Photos / Video / Recording

You must have consent before photographing, videotaping or audio recording any participants at Coracle.
